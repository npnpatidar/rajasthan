### **Topic 1: India’s Space Sector Reforms & Gaganyaan Mission**

*   **Indian Space Policy 2023:**
    *   Aims to formalize the role of the Department of Space and enhance the participation of the private sector (Non-Governmental Entities - NGEs).
    *   ISRO will focus primarily on Research & Development (R&D) and high-end technology, transitioning routine production to the industry.
*   **Gaganyaan Mission Overview:**
    *   **Objective:** To demonstrate human spaceflight capability by launching a 3-member crew to an orbit of 400 km for a 3-day mission and returning them safely to Earth.
    *   **Launch Vehicle:** LVM3 (Launch Vehicle Mark-3) is being upgraded to human-rated standards (HLVM3).
    *   **Key Modules:** 
        *   **Crew Module:** Pressurized habitat for the astronauts.
        *   **Service Module:** Provides power, propulsion, and life support systems.
*   **Safety Protocols:**
    *   **Crew Escape System (CES):** Designed to pull the crew module away to a safe distance in case of a launch failure.
    *   **TV-D1 Mission:** Recent successful test demonstrating the separation of the crew module and the deployment of parachutes.
*   **Vyommitra:** A female-looking humanoid robot developed by ISRO to fly on uncrewed missions to monitor system parameters and simulate human functions.

### **Topic 2: National Green Hydrogen Mission (NGHM)**

*   **Primary Goals (Target 2030):**
    *   Development of green hydrogen production capacity of at least 5 MMT (Million Metric Tonnes) per annum.
    *   Addition of about 125 GW of renewable energy capacity.
    *   Reduction in fossil fuel imports worth over ₹1 lakh crore.
    *   Abatement of nearly 50 MMT of annual greenhouse gas emissions.
*   **SIGHT Program:**
    *   Stands for **Strategic Interventions for Green Hydrogen Transition**.
    *   Provides financial incentives for the domestic manufacturing of electrolyzers.
    *   Offers performance-linked incentives for the production of green hydrogen.
*   **Hydrogen Categorization:**

| Type | Production Method | Carbon Footprint |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Green Hydrogen** | Electrolysis of water using renewable energy (Solar/Wind). | Zero/Negligible |
| **Blue Hydrogen** | Derived from natural gas with Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS). | Low |
| **Grey Hydrogen** | Derived from natural gas (methane) without carbon capture. | High |

### **Topic 3: Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023**

*   **Key Definitions:**
    *   **Data Principal:** The individual to whom the personal data relates.
    *   **Data Fiduciary:** The entity (company or government) that determines the purpose and means of processing personal data.
*   **Consent Framework:**
    *   Personal data can only be processed for a lawful purpose after obtaining the "free, specific, informed, and unambiguous" consent of the Data Principal.
    *   A notice must be provided in English or any language specified in the 8th Schedule of the Constitution.
*   **Rights of the Data Principal:**
    *   Right to access information about their data.
    *   Right to correction and erasure of data.
    *   Right to grievance redressal.
    *   Right to nominate a person to exercise rights in case of death or incapacity.
*   **Data Protection Board of India:**
    *   A specialized body to be established by the Central Government to monitor compliance and resolve disputes.
*   **Penalties:**
    *   Financial penalties for data breaches can go up to ₹250 crore, depending on the severity of the violation.

### **Topic 4: RBI’s Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) Updates**

*   **Current Stance:** Focus on "Withdrawal of Accommodation" to ensure that inflation progressively aligns with the target while supporting growth.
*   **Inflation Targeting:** The primary goal remains 4% CPI inflation with a tolerance band of +/- 2%.
*   **Key Policy Rates:**
    *   **Repo Rate:** The rate at which RBI lends money to commercial banks (currently 6.50%).
    *   **Reverse Repo Rate:** The rate at which RBI borrows money from banks.
    *   **MSF (Marginal Standing Facility):** The rate for overnight emergency loans to banks.
*   **Urban Co-operative Banks (UCBs):** New guidelines issued to improve credit flow to priority sectors and enhance governance standards.
