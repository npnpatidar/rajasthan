### 16th BRICS Summit (October 2024)

**Summit Overview**
*   **Host City:** Kazan, Russia.
*   **Theme:** "Strengthening Multilateralism for Just Global Development and Security."
*   **Significance:** First summit following the 2024 expansion (inclusion of Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, and UAE).
*   **Primary Outcome:** Adoption of the **Kazan Declaration**, which emphasizes a more representative and multipolar world order.

**Introduction of "Partner Country" Status**
*   The summit established a new "Partner Country" category instead of immediate full membership for new applicants.
*   **13 Partner Countries:** Algeria, Belarus, Bolivia, Cuba, Indonesia, Kazakhstan, Malaysia, Nigeria, Thailand, Turkey, Uganda, Uzbekistan, and Vietnam.

**Economic and Financial Initiatives**
*   **BRICS Clear:** A proposed independent system for settlement and clearing to facilitate cross-border trade in grain and other commodities.
*   **BRICS Pay:** Discussion on a decentralized messaging and payment system to reduce reliance on the Western-led SWIFT network.
*   **Local Currencies:** Emphasis on using local currencies in financial transactions between member states to mitigate global financial risks.

*   **Five Pillars of Cooperation:** PM Modi emphasized Trade, Health, Technology, Energy, and People-to-People ties.
*   **LAC Patrolling Agreement:** The summit provided the backdrop for the first formal bilateral meeting between India and China in five years, following a consensus on patrolling rights along the Line of Actual Control (LAC).
*   **Stance on Terrorism:** India pushed for the early adoption of the Comprehensive Convention on International Terrorism (CCIT) at the UN.

**Global Issues and Geopolitics**
*   **Conflict Resolution:** The Kazan Declaration called for the peaceful resolution of conflicts through diplomacy, specifically addressing the situations in Ukraine and the Middle East (Gaza and Lebanon).
*   **UN Reforms:** Renewed calls for the reform of the UN Security Council to include greater representation from the Global South.
