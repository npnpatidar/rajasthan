### Interpol Operations
*   Interpol launched "Operation Thunder" to rescue trafficked animals globally, saving 3000 live animals including tigers, sea turtles, birds, and pangolins (the world's most trafficked animal).
*   Interpol was established on September 7, 1923, with its headquarters in Lyon, France. International Police Day is celebrated on September 7.
*   Other Indian operations: Operation Sagar Bandhu (aid to Sri Lanka cyclone), Operation Brahma (aid to Myanmar earthquake), Operation Sindoor (against Pakistan, launched May 7), Operation Shiv Shakti/Keller/Mahadev (counter-terrorism in J&K), Operation Rising Lion (Israel's attack on Iran), Operation Spider Web (Ukraine's attack on Russia), Operation Sindhu (rescue of Indians from Iran).
*   K.G.S. Dhillon authored the book 'Operation Sindoor'.

### Infrastructure & Environment
*   National Highways Authority of India (NHAI) built India's first wildlife-safe road on NH 45 in Madhya Pradesh, connecting Bhopal to Jabalpur.
*   The road's Hiransindur section (approx. 12 km) passes through Veerangana Durgavati Tiger Reserve and Norodehi Wildlife Sanctuary.
*   Features include red markings on the road instead of speed breakers to slow down vehicles, utilizing the high wavelength of red for visibility.
*   Cheetah habitats in Madhya Pradesh include Kuno National Park, Gandhi Sagar, and Norodehi.

### Indian Navy
*   The Indian Navy will commission its second MH-60R helicopter squadron, INS 335 "Osprey," in Goa on December 17.
*   The commissioning will be conducted by Dinesh Tripathi, the Head of the Indian Navy, on INS Hansa.

### Cultural Heritage & Recognition
*   Italy became the first country to receive UNESCO recognition for all its national cuisines.
    *   Italy has the highest number of UNESCO World Heritage Sites globally.
    *   Italian PM Giorgia Meloni's autobiography includes a foreword by Indian PM Narendra Modi.
*   Ellora Caves (Maharashtra, formerly Aurangabad, now Chhatrapati Sambhaji Nagar) were included in UNESCO World Heritage list in 1983. They have 34 caves related to Brahmanism, Buddhism, and Jainism, and are depicted on the ₹20 banknote.
*   Petra (Jordan) is known as the "Rose City," partly carved into rock.
*   Diwali was included as India's 16th Intangible Cultural Heritage by UNESCO.
*   72 Learning Cities were also recently included by UNESCO.

### Commemorations & Social Initiatives
*   Vijay Diwas 2025 was celebrated on December 16, marking the 54th anniversary of India's victory over Pakistan in the 1971 war, leading to Bangladesh's liberation.
    *   Sheikh Mujibur Rahman played a crucial role in Bangladesh's independence.
    *   Pakistan officially surrendered on December 16, 1971, after a 13-day conflict.
    *   Indian Army showcased its 'Sambhav' communication system and 'Ekam AI' platform (for sensitive environments).
*   International Literacy Day was celebrated on September 8.
*   The Ministry of Textiles launched the 'Chaupal' program in New Delhi to empower handicrafts artisans. Giriraj Singh is the Textile Minister.
*   The Maharashtra government banned physical and mental punishment in schools, emphasizing provisions of the RTE Act 2009 (Sections 16 & 17).
*   The ban includes verbal abuse, taunting, objectionable language, and discrimination.
*   Teachers are also restricted from non-academic interaction via personal messages/social media and using student photos/videos without parental consent.
