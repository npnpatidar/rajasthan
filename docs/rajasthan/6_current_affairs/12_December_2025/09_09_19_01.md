### Rajasthan State Information Commission

*   **Appointment:** Sunil Sharma has been appointed as an Information Commissioner.
*   **Establishment:** The Commission was established on April 18, 2006, under Section 15 of the Right to Information Act, 2005.
*   **Nature:** It is a statutory body.
*   **Composition:** It can have 1 Chief Information Commissioner and up to 10 Information Commissioners.
*   **First Chief Information Commissioner:** M.D. Korani.
*   **Current Chief Information Commissioner:** M.L. Lather.
*   **Appointment Process:** Commissioners are appointed by the Governor based on the recommendations of a selection committee comprising the Chief Minister, Leader of Opposition, and a Cabinet Minister.
*   **Term:** The term of office is 3 years or until 65 years of age.
*   **Role:** It serves as the final appellate authority under the RTI Act, 2005, in Rajasthan.
*   **Penalties:** The Commission can impose fines ranging from ₹250 to ₹25,000 for non-compliance with RTI requests.

### Gang Canal Centenary Celebration

*   **Launch Date:** December 5, 2025.
*   **Historical Context:**
    *   Known as Rajasthan's first canal.
    *   Foundation laid by Maharaja Ganga Singh on December 5, 1925, earning him the title 'Bhagirath of Modern India'.
    *   Construction completed in 1927.
    *   Inaugurated on October 26, 1927, by Lord Irwin.
    *   Primarily benefits Ganganagar (most) and Hanumangarh districts.
*   **Centenary Event Details:**
    *   **Venue:** Gajar Mandi, Sadhuwali, Ganganagar.
    *   **Chief Guest:** The Chief Minister.
    *   **Announcements:**
        *   ₹1717 crore sanctioned for the modernization of the Gang Canal.

    *   **Tagline:** "Gang Nahar Samriddhi ki Aviral Dhara" (Gang Canal: Continuous Flow of Prosperity).

### Rajasthan Dead Body Act

*   **Official Name:** Rajasthan Dead Body Respect Bill 2023.
*   **Passage & Enactment:**
    *   Passed on July 20, 2023.
    *   Received Governor's assent on August 17, 2023.
    *   Notified on August 18, 2023.
*   **Significance:** Rajasthan is the first state in India to implement such a law regarding the respect of deceased bodies.
*   **Key Provisions:**
    *   **Failure to Take Custody:** If family members fail to take custody of a deceased's body, they may face up to one year imprisonment, a fine, or both.
    *   **Protesting with Dead Body (by Family):** Using a dead body for protest on the road by family members can lead to imprisonment from six months to two years and/or a fine.
    *   **Protesting with Dead Body (by Others):** If individuals other than family use a dead body for protest, they can face imprisonment from six months to five years and/or a fine.
    *   **Leaking Genetic Data:** Employees or officers found leaking genetic data of unclaimed bodies face imprisonment of 3 to 10 years.
    *   **Mandatory Last Rites:** Following a magistrate's order, if family members do not perform the last rites within 24 hours, the police are authorized to do so.
