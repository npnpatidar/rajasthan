### Constitutional Amendments

*   **General Information:**
    *   Constitutional amendments are outlined in Part 20, Article 368 of the Constitution, adopted from South Africa.
    *   106 constitutional amendment acts have been passed to date.
    *   The 129th Constitutional Amendment Bill was presented for 'One Nation One Election'.
*   **First Amendment (1951):** Added Schedule 9 to the Constitution, related to land reforms.
*   **Seventh Amendment (1956):** Related to the State Reorganisation Commission.
*   **21st Amendment (1967):** Added Sindhi as the 15th language to Schedule 8 of the Constitution.
*   **26th Amendment (1971):** Abolished the Privy Purse (privileges to former rulers).
*   **36th Amendment (1975):** Granted Sikkim full statehood within India.
*   **42nd Amendment (1976) - "Mini Constitution":**
    *   Amended the Preamble for the first time, adding "Socialist," "Secular," and "Integrity" words.
    *   Added Part IVA (Fundamental Duties, Article 51A) based on the Sardar Swaran Singh Committee recommendations.
    *   Added Article 39A (Free Legal Aid).
    *   Added Article 48A (Environmental and Forest Protection).
    *   Added Part XIVA (Tribunals, Articles 323A and 323B).
    *   Transferred five subjects (Education, Forests, Wildlife, Weights & Measures, Administration of Justice) from the State List to the Concurrent List.
*   **44th Amendment (1978):** Removed the Right to Property from the list of Fundamental Rights, making it a legal right under Article 300A.
*   **52nd Amendment (1985):** Added Schedule 10 to the Constitution, related to the Anti-defection Law.
*   **61st Amendment (1989):** Reduced the voting age from 21 years to 18 years.
*   **69th Amendment (1991):** Granted Delhi the status of National Capital Region (NCR) and provided for a Legislative Assembly for Delhi with a 10% limit on the size of the Council of Ministers (Article 239AA).
*   **70th Amendment (1992):** Included elected members of the Delhi and Puducherry Legislative Assemblies in the Electoral College for the President's election.
*   **71st Amendment (1992):** Added three new languages (Nepali, Manipuri, Konkani) to Schedule 8, increasing the total to 18 languages.
*   **73rd Amendment (1992):**
    *   Added Part IX (Panchayati Raj) and Articles 243 to 243O.
    *   Added Schedule 11 (29 subjects) related to rural local self-government.
    *   Implemented on April 24, 1993.
*   **74th Amendment (1992):** Added Part IXA (Urban Local Bodies) and Schedule 12.
*   **86th Amendment (2002):**
    *   Added Article 21A, making the Right to Education a Fundamental Right.
    *   Added the 11th Fundamental Duty (Article 51A(k)) regarding parental duty to provide education to children.
*   **89th Amendment (2003):** Created the National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST) by inserting Article 338A, separating it from the National Commission for Scheduled Castes.
*   **91st Amendment (2003):** Limited the size of the Council of Ministers at the Centre and in States to 15% of the total members of the Lok Sabha/Legislative Assembly, respectively (with an exception for Delhi at 10%).
*   **92nd Amendment (2003):** Added four new languages (Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, Santhali) to Schedule 8, making the total 22 languages.
*   **97th Amendment (2011):** Added Part IXB (Cooperative Societies) and Article 43B.
*   **99th Amendment (2014):** Established the National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC), which was later declared unconstitutional by the Supreme Court.
*   **100th Amendment (2015):** Related to the Land Boundary Agreement (Tin Bigha Corridor) between India and Bangladesh.
*   **101st Amendment (2016):** Introduced the Goods and Services Tax (GST), implemented on July 1, 2017.
*   **102nd Amendment (2018):** Granted constitutional status to the National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC) by inserting Article 338B.
*   **103rd Amendment (2019):** Provided 10% reservation for Economically Weaker Sections (EWS) by inserting Articles 15(6) and 16(6).
*   **104th Amendment (2019):** Extended reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in the Lok Sabha and State Assemblies by 10 years and abolished the reservation for Anglo-Indians.
*   **105th Amendment (2021):** Restored the power of states to identify and specify socially and educationally backward classes (OBCs).
*   **106th Amendment (2023):** "Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam" provides 33% reservation for women in the Lok Sabha and State Legislative Assemblies (to be implemented after delimitation and census).
*   **129th Constitutional Amendment Bill:** Presented for 'One Nation One Election', associated with the Ram Nath Kovind Committee.
