## Nobel Prizes

### Nobel Prize History and Background
*   **Establishment:** Began in 1901, based on the will of Alfred Nobel (inventor of dynamite) drafted in 1895. Nobel died in 1896.
*   **Fields:** Awarded in six fields.
*   **Economics Prize:** Started later in 1968, first awarded in 1969. Established by Sweden's Sveriges Riksbank (also known as the Riksbank Award).
*   **Award Location:** All prizes are awarded in Stockholm, Sweden, except for the Peace Prize, which is awarded in Oslo, Norway.
*   **Award Date:** Announced earlier but awarded on December 10, commemorating Alfred Nobel's death anniversary.

### Nobel Prize 2025 Winners

*   **Physiology or Medicine:**
    *   **Winners:** Mary Bronkov, Friends Remesdel, Simons Sakaguchi.
    *   **Discovery:** For their work on immune system tolerance.
    *   **First Award (1901):** Adolf von Behring.
*   **Physics:**
    *   **Winners:** Michel S. Debourette, John Clarke, Martinis.
    *   **Discovery:** For macroscopic quantum mechanical tunneling in electrical circuits.
    *   **First Award (1901):** Wilhelm Conrad Röntgen (for the discovery of X-rays).
*   **Chemistry:**
    *   **Winners:** Richard Robson, Umar Yagi, Susumu Kitagawa.
    *   **Discovery:** For their work on Metal-Organic Frameworks (MOFs).
    *   **First Award (1901):** Jacobus Henricus van 't Hoff.
*   **Literature:**
    *   **Winner:** László Krasznahorkai (Hungarian author).
    *   **Reason:** For his captivating and visionary works.
    *   **First Award (1901):** Sully Prudhomme.
*   **Peace:**
    *   **Winner:** María Corina Machado (Venezuelan opposition leader).
    *   **First Award (1901):** Henry Dunant (founder of Red Cross) and Frédéric Passy.
    *   **Note:** The Red Cross has received the Peace Prize three times.
*   **Economics:**
    *   **Winners:** Joel Mokyr, Philippe Aghion, Peter Howitt.
    *   **Discovery:** For identifying necessary conditions for sustainable development through technological progress and the theory of creative destruction.
    *   **First Award (1969):** Ragnar Frisch and Jan Tinbergen.

### Indian Nobel Laureates
*   **Rabindranath Tagore:** 1913, Literature, for "Gitanjali". First Indian and first Asian to win in Literature.
*   **C.V. Raman:** 1930, Physics, for the Raman Effect (scattering of light).
*   **Har Gobind Khorana:** 1968, Medicine, for artificial gene synthesis and explanation of the genetic code in protein synthesis.
*   **Mother Teresa:** 1979, Peace. First Indian woman to win.
*   **Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar:** 1983, Physics, for the Chandrasekhar Limit (theoretical studies of physical processes important to the structure and evolution of stars).
*   **Amartya Sen:** 1998, Economics, for his contributions to welfare economics (concept of public welfare state). First Indian to win in Economics.
*   **V.S. Naipaul:** 2001, Literature, for "In a Free State".
*   **Venkatraman Ramakrishnan:** 2009, Chemistry, for his work on the structure of ribosomes.
*   **Kailash Satyarthi:** 2014, Peace, for "Bachpan Bachao Andolan" (shared with Malala Yousafzai).
*   **Abhijit Banerjee:** 2019, Economics, for his experimental approach to alleviating global poverty.

## Budget 2025-26 (India)

### Budget History (India)
*   **First Budget:** 1860, by James Wilson (considered the father of the Indian Budget).
*   **First Post-Independence Budget:** K. Shanmukham Chetty.
*   **First Republic India Budget:** John Mathai.
*   **Most Budgets Presented:** Morarji Desai (10 times).
*   **First Woman to Present Budget:** Indira Gandhi.
*   **First Full-time Female Finance Minister:** Nirmala Sitharaman.
*   **Longest Budget Speech:** Nirmala Sitharaman (2020).

### Budget 2025-26 Highlights
*   **Presented By:** Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman for the 8th time on February 1.
*   **Vision of Developed India:**
    *   Freedom from poverty.
    *   100% school education.
    *   Excellent, affordable, and accessible healthcare facilities.
    *   100% skilled workforce.
    *   70% women's participation in economic activity.
    *   India as the "Food Basket of the World".
*   **Four Engines of Growth:**
    *   **Destination:** Developed India.
    *   **Guidance:** Inclusivity.
    *   **Fuel:** Reforms.
    *   **Engines:** Agriculture, MSME, Investment, Export.

#### Agriculture (First Engine)
*   **Prime Minister Dhana-Dhanya Krishi Yojana:** Launched in 100 districts to increase agricultural productivity, benefiting 1.7 crore farmers.
*   **Self-Sufficiency in Pulses Mission:** 6-year mission focusing on Tur (Arhar), Urad, and Masoor.
*   **Shri Anna (Millets) Mission:** Continued focus (2023 was the International Year of Millets).
*   **Makhana Board:** To be established in Bihar.
*   **National High-Yield Seed Mission:** For developing high-yielding seeds.
*   **Cotton Production Mission:** 5-year mission to produce high-quality cotton for the textile industry.
*   **Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Limit:** Increased from ₹3 lakh to ₹5 lakh.
*   **Urea Plant:** To be established in Namrup, Assam, with a production capacity of 12.7 lakh metric tons.

#### MSME (Second Engine)
*   **Revised Definitions (based on Investment & Turnover):**
    *   **Micro:** Investment up to ₹2.5 crore; Turnover up to ₹10 crore.
    *   **Small:** Investment up to ₹25 crore; Turnover up to ₹100 crore.
    *   **Medium:** Investment up to ₹125 crore; Turnover up to ₹500 crore.
*   **Credit Card for Micro Enterprises:** ₹5 lakh limit, available through the Udhyam Portal.
*   **National Manufacturing Mission:** To promote 'Make in India' in footwear, toys, and food processing industries.

#### Investment (Third Engine)
*   **Atal Tinkering Labs:** 500 new labs to be established in government schools over the next 5 years to boost interest in science, technology, and mathematics.
*   **Indian Language Book Scheme:** Digital books in Indian languages for school and higher education.
*   **National Skill Excellence Centers:** Five centers to be established to promote 'Make for India, Make for the World'.
*   **AI in Education:** ₹500 crore allocated for AI excellence centers in education.
*   **Cancer Care:** Cancer medicines made cheaper; Day Care Cancer Hospitals to be established in all district hospitals within 3 years.
*   **Jal Jeevan Mission:** Extended until 2028 (originally set to end in 2024).
*   **Maritime Development Fund:** Government to contribute 49% of the funds.
*   **UDAN Scheme:** 120 new destinations to be connected over the next 10 years.
*   **Greenfield Airport:** To be established in Bihar.
*   **West Kosi Canal Project:** To be developed in Mithilanchal, Bihar.
*   **Gyan Bharatam Mission:** To preserve over 1 crore ancient manuscripts.

#### Export (Fourth Engine)
*   **Bharat Trade Net Platform:** Launched to improve supply chain efficiency for exports.

#### Reforms (Fuel)
*   **FDI in Insurance Sector:** Foreign Direct Investment limit increased from 74% to 100%.

### Tax Reforms
*   **Fiscal Deficit (Estimated):**
    *   2024-25: 4.8% of GDP.
    *   2025-26: 4.4% of GDP.
*   **Tax Exemption:** Tax removed from 36 life-saving drugs (including those for rare diseases and cancer).
*   **New Income Tax Regime:** A new income tax bill will be introduced, replacing the 1961 act.
*   **Revised Tax Slabs (New Regime):** Income up to ₹12 lakh is tax-free.
    *   ₹0-4 lakh: 0%
    *   ₹4-8 lakh: 5%
    *   ₹8-12 lakh: 10%
    *   ₹12-16 lakh: 15%
    *   ₹16-20 lakh: 20%
    *   ₹20-24 lakh: 25%
    *   Above ₹24 lakh: 30%

### Revenue and Expenditure
*   **Sources of Revenue (Where money comes from):**
    1.  Borrowings: 24%
    2.  Income Tax: 22%
    3.  GST: 18%
*   **Areas of Expenditure (Where money goes):**
    1.  State's Share in Taxes & Duties: 22%
    2.  Interest Payments: 20%
*   **Top Spending Sectors:**
    1.  Defense
    2.  Rural Development
    3.  Home Ministry
    4.  Agriculture
