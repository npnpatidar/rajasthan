### Rajasthan Development & Infrastructure

*   **Inland Port Waterway:**
    *   An Inland Port Waterway will be constructed in Jalore.
    *   Previously, an Inland Container Depot/Port was announced for Bakhasar, Shahgarh, Barmer, planned to connect to the Gulf of Kutch via a canal.
    *   A recent MoU declared the Jawai, Luni, and Rann of Kutch region as National Water Highway (NWH) No. 48.
    *   Under NWH 48, Jalore will be connected to Kandla Port via a canal.
    *   The canal will be 262 km long, with 12 km within Rajasthan.
    *   The survey for this canal is being conducted by IIT Madras.
    *   Ramgarh, Jaisalmer: Sighting of the rare Asiatic Caracal cat.
    *   Bakhasar, Shahgarh, Barmer: Besides the proposed inland port, National Highway 925A features an emergency landing strip, which recently saw fighter jets and transport aircraft landings during exercises.
    *   Khichan, Phalodi: Has been included as a Ramsar Site.

### Jaipur City Updates

*   **Jaipur Foundation Day:**
    *   Celebrated on November 18, 2025, with the theme "Vikas bhi aur Virasat bhi" (Development and Heritage) at Statue Circle.
    *   Jaipur was founded on November 18, 1727, by Sawai Jai Singh, with Vidyadhar Bhattacharya as its architect. It is designed on a 90-degree grid with nine sectors.
    *   **Upcoming 300th Anniversary (2027):** The state budget 2025-26 announced year-long celebrations for Jaipur's 300th foundation anniversary in 2027.
    *   A "Govind Dev Ji Kala Mahotsav" will be organized with a budget of ₹50 crore.
    *   **Upanama (Nicknames):** Jaipur is known as Pink City, Paris of the East, Island of Glory, Heritage City, Second Kashi of Rajasthan, Ratna Nagari, and Panna Nagari.
*   **Central Sanskrit University:** Hosted the West Central Regional Youth Festival from November 12-14, 2025.
    *   (Note: Jaipur has two Sanskrit universities: Central Sanskrit University, established by the Government of India, and Jagadguru Ramanandacharya Sanskrit University, a state government university.)
*   **Jagadguru Ramanandacharya Sanskrit University:** Hosted the State Level Van Mahotsav (Forest Festival) on July 27, 2025.
    *   Amrita Devi Van Awards were distributed, recognizing:
        *   Van (Forest) & Wildlife Protection: Upkar Sansthan, Alwar.
        *   Forest Protection: Murari Dan Barath (Jalore) and Ajit Singh (Sikar).
        *   Wildlife Protection: Gauri Shankar Sharma and Govind Bhardwaj.
*   **PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana:** The state-level ceremony for the 21st installment of the scheme was held in Jaipur at SIAM (State Institute of Agricultural Management) in Durgapura.
    *   PM Kisan Utsav Diwas was observed on November 19, 2025.
    *   The PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Yojana, launched in February 2019 (with benefits starting December 2018), provides ₹6,000 annually to farmers in three installments of ₹2,000 each.
    *   The national-level program for the 21st installment was held in Coimbatore, distributing ₹18,000 crore to 9 crore farmers nationwide.
    *   In Rajasthan, 66.62 lakh farmers received ₹1332.40 crore.
    *   SIAM (established 1993) has also initiated an Organic Mart.
*   **Lord Parshvanath Crystal Idol:** Jaipur is home to the country's most expensive Lord Parshvanath crystal idol, crafted from crystals sourced from Afghanistan and Brazil, embedded with gems, and designed for 360-degree viewing.
*   **Jaipur Airport Lion Sculpture:** A 30-foot-high lion sculpture is being constructed at Jaipur Airport by artist Pawan Kumar Bhatt.
