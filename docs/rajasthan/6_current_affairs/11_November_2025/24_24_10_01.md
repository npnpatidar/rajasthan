**Budget 2024-25 Overview**
*   **Historical Budget Facts:**
    *   First budget in India: James Wilson (1860).
    *   First post-independence budget: K. Shanmukham Chetty.
    *   First Republic India budget: John Mathai.
    *   Most budgets presented: Morarji Desai (10 times).
    *   First female to present budget: Indira Gandhi.
    *   First full-time female Finance Minister: Nirmala Sitharaman.
    *   Longest budget speech: Nirmala Sitharaman (2020).
    *   Nirmala Sitharaman presented the budget for the 8th time on February 1st.
*   **Vision for Developed India (Viksit Bharat):**
    *   Freedom from poverty.
    *   100% schooling.
    *   Affordable and accessible healthcare.
    *   100% skilled workforce.
    *   70% female participation in economic activity.
    *   India as the "Food Basket of the World."
*   **Budget 2024-25 Framework:**
    *   Destination: Developed India.
    *   Guidance: Inclusivity.
    *   Fuel: Reforms.
    *   Four Engines: Agriculture, MSME, Investment, Export.

**Key Initiatives and Policies**
*   **Agriculture Sector:**
    *   **Prime Minister Dhan Dhanya Krishi Yojana:** Aims to increase agricultural productivity, launched in 100 districts, benefiting 1.7 crore farmers.
    *   **Pulses Self-Sufficiency Mission:** A 6-year mission focusing on Toor/Arhar, Urad, and Masoor pulses.
    *   Missions for vegetables, fruits, and Shree Ann (millets) also initiated. (2023 was declared the "Year of Millets").
    *   **Makhana Board:** To be established in Bihar.
    *   National High-Yield Seed Mission introduced.
    *   **Cotton Production Mission:** A 5-year mission to boost quality cotton production.
    *   **Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Limit:** Increased from ₹3 lakh to ₹5 lakh.
    *   **Urea Plant:** To be established in Namrup, Assam, with a 12.7 lakh metric ton production capacity.
*   **MSME Sector:**
    *   **Revised MSME Definitions (Investment / Turnover):**
        *   Micro: Up to ₹2.5 crore / Up to ₹10 crore.
        *   Small: Up to ₹25 crore / Up to ₹100 crore.
        *   Medium: Up to ₹125 crore / Up to ₹500 crore.
    *   **Credit Card for Micro Enterprises:** ₹5 lakh limit, accessible via the Udyam Portal.
    *   **National Manufacturing Mission:** To promote "Make in India" in footwear, toys, and food processing industries.
*   **Investment & Infrastructure:**
    *   **Atal Tinkering Labs:** 500 labs to be set up in government schools over 5 years to promote STEM education and research.
    *   **Bharatiya Bhasha Pustak Scheme:** Digital books in Indian languages for school and higher education.
    *   Five **National Skill Excellence Centers** to be established for "Make for India and Make for the World."
    *   **AI Promotion in Education:** Establishment of an Excellence Center for AI, allocated ₹500 crore.
    *   **Cancer Care:** Cancer medicines made cheaper; one day-care cancer hospital planned for every district hospital within 3 years.
    *   **Jal Jeevan Mission:** Extended until 2028 (initially 2019-2024).
    *   **Maritime Development Fund:** Government to contribute 49%.
    *   **UDAAN Scheme:** 120 new destinations to be explored for air connectivity in the next 10 years.
    *   **Bihar Projects:** Greenfield Airport and the West Kosi Canal Project in Mithilanchal.
    *   **Gyan Bharatam Mission:** Launched for the preservation of over 1 crore manuscripts.
*   **Export Sector:**
    *   **India Trade Net Platform:** Launched to enhance export supply chain.
*   **Reforms:**
    *   **Insurance Sector FDI Limit:** Increased from 74% to 100%.

**Economic & Financial Data**
*   **Fiscal Deficit Estimates:**
    *   FY 2024-25: 4.8% of GDP.
    *   FY 2025-26: 4.4% of GDP.
*   **Taxation:**
    *   Tax exempted on 36 life-saving drugs (for rare diseases, cancer).
    *   A new Income Tax Bill to replace the 1961 act.
    *   **Revised Income Tax Slabs:**
        *   Up to ₹12 lakh: Tax-free.
        *   ₹0-4 lakh: 0%
        *   ₹4-8 lakh: 5%
        *   ₹8-12 lakh: 10%
        *   ₹12-16 lakh: 15%
        *   ₹16-20 lakh: 20%
        *   ₹20-24 lakh: 25%
        *   Above ₹24 lakh: 30%
*   **Sources of Government Revenue:**
    *   1st: Borrowings (24%)
    *   2nd: Income Tax (22%)
    *   3rd: GST (18%)
*   **Government Expenditure Priorities:**
    *   1st: States' Share in Taxes & Duties (22%)
    *   2nd: Interest Payments (20%)
    *   **Sectoral Spending:** 1st: Defense, 2nd: Rural Development, 3rd: Home Ministry, 4th: Agriculture & Allied Activities.
