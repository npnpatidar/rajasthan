**Rajasthan Global Capability Policy 2025**

*   Approved on November 19, 2025, during a state cabinet meeting.
*   **Target:** Establish over 200 Global Capability Centers (GCCs) in Rajasthan by 2030.
    *   **Definition of GCC:** A center opened by a multinational company (MNC) in another country (e.g., an American company opening a center in India) to leverage talent and expand operations.
*   **Proposed locations for GCC hubs:** Jaipur, Jodhpur, Udaipur.
*   **Employment generation target:** 1.5 lakh jobs.
*   **Benefits offered under RIIPS 2024 (Rajasthan Investment Promotion Scheme 2024, effective December 4, 2024):**
    *   Capital Investment Subsidy: 30% of project cost or maximum ₹10 crore.
    *   Land/Lease Cost Reimbursement: 50% of cost or maximum ₹1 crore.
    *   Employee Skill Development: 50% of cost.
    *   Payroll Subsidy: 30% of employee salary or maximum ₹1.25 lakh annually per employee for the first 3 years.
    *   Rental Subsidy: 50% for the first 3 years, 25% for the next 2 years, with a maximum of ₹5 crore.

**Rajasthan Rajya Vidyut Utpadan Nigam Limited (RRVUNL) MoU**

*   RRVUNL has signed an MoU with Singareni Collieries Company Limited (SCCL) for energy projects.
*   **Background of RRVUNL:**
    *   Rajasthan Rajya Vidyut Mandal established on July 1, 1957.
    *   Based on the Rajasthan Rajya Vidyut Sudhar Adhiniyam 1999 (effective June 2000), the Mandal was divided into five entities on July 19, 2000:
        *   Jaipur Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited (JVVNL) - HQ Jaipur
        *   Ajmer Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited (AVVNL) - HQ Ajmer
        *   Jodhpur Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited (JdVVNL) - HQ Jodhpur
        *   Rajasthan Vidyut Prasaran Nigam Limited (RVPNL) - HQ Jaipur
        *   Rajasthan Rajya Vidyut Utpadan Nigam Limited (RRVUNL) - HQ Jaipur
*   **Projects under MoU with SCCL:**
    *   An 800 MW coal-based power project in Telangana.
    *   A 1500 MW solar power project in Pugal, Bikaner (land also allocated in Rawatbhata, Chittorgarh).
*   **Partnership share:** SCCL 74%, RRVUNL 26%.

**Jaisalmer Fossil Park and Geo-Tourism**

*   Jaisalmer's Fossil Park is slated to be developed as a geo-tourism destination.
*   **Key tourism spots in Jaisalmer:**
    *   Aakal Wood Fossil Park: Contains evidence of dinosaurs and marine vegetation from 18-25 crore years ago.
    *   Sam Village: Known for its vegetation-less landscape.
    *   Longewala: Site of a War Museum.
    *   Khuhri Village: Famous for its sand dunes.
    *   Jaisalmer Fort, Tanot Mata Temple, Pokaran (where Indira Shakti Panorama is located).
*   **Khamba Fort (Jaisalmer):** Budget 2024-25 announced the establishment of an Open Rock Museum here, to be developed on the lines of Chandigarh's Rock Garden. However, this project is currently stalled due to departmental disputes.
