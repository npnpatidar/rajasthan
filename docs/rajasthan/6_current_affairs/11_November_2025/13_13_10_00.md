**Current Affairs Updates**

*   **Ozone Layer Protection Meeting (MOP 37)**
    *   The 37th Meeting of the Parties to the Montreal Protocol on Substances that Deplete the Ozone Layer (MOP 37) was held in Nairobi, Kenya.
    *   The Montreal Protocol, signed on September 16, 1987, aims to protect the ozone layer by phasing out ozone-depleting substances like Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs, or Freon).
    *   The first MOP (MOP 1) took place in Helsinki, Finland, in 1989.
    *   The headquarters of the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) are in Nairobi.
    *   COP 30 is currently underway in Belém, Brazil.
    *   COP 32 is confirmed to be held in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.

*   **Climate Risk Index 2026**
    *   The Climate Risk Index 2026 was released by German Watch, an organization based in Bonn, Germany.
    *   The index evaluates extreme weather events from 1995 to 2024.
    *   Globally, approximately 9,700 extreme weather events occurred during this period, resulting in about 8.32 million deaths.
    *   Dominica is identified as the most affected country.
    *   India is ranked 9th among the most affected countries.
    *   India ranks 3rd in terms of population affected by these events.

*   **Military Exercise "Vinbax 25"**
    *   The 6th edition of the "Vinbax 25" military exercise took place between the armies of India and Vietnam.
    *   The exercise was held in Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam.
    *   Other recent military exercises:
        *   **Sagar Kavach:** Conducted by the Indian Coast Guard off the coasts of Karnataka and Kerala.
        *   **Brahmashira:** Part of the larger "Trishul" exercise, held in the Rann of Kutch and Sir Creek sector of Gujarat, involving the Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force.
        *   **Maru Jwala / Akhand Prahar:** Held in the Thar Desert region of India (Rajasthan).

*   **Earthshot Prize 2025**
    *   The winners of the Earthshot Prize 2025 were announced in Rio, Brazil.
    *   The award recognizes efforts in combating climate change.
    *   Notable winners include:
        *   **Re.green (Brazil):** For Amazon forest conservation.
        *   **Omamuyemi Akeler (Nigeria):** For contributions to the industry sector.
        *   **Carlos Fernando (Bogotá, Colombia):** For clean air initiatives.
        *   **Rebecca Hubbard:** For work related to the High Seas Treaty.
        *   **Roona Khan (Bangladesh).**

*   **6th National Water Awards 2024**
    *   The 6th National Water Awards, recognizing efforts in water conservation, were announced.
    *   Maharashtra received the "Best State" award.
    *   Gujarat secured the second position, followed by Haryana in third.
    *   Navi Mumbai was awarded as the "Best Urban Local Body."
    *   The awards were announced by Union Minister of Jal Shakti, C.R. Patil, from Delhi.

*   **OICA Leadership**
    *   Sailesh Chandra became the first Indian to lead the Organisation Internationale des Constructeurs d'Automobiles (OICA).
    *   OICA is an international trade association representing the automotive industry, established in 1919.

*   **National Public Service Broadcasting Day 2025**
    *   Observed annually on November 12th.
    *   Commemorates Mahatma Gandhi's only address to the nation via All India Radio from Delhi on November 12, 1947.
    *   Gandhi's address was aimed at displaced refugees following the partition of India and Pakistan.
    *   National Broadcasting Day is celebrated on July 23rd, marking the start of radio broadcasting in India from Bombay in 1927.

*   **Modified Bharat Net Project**
    *   Punjab has become the first state to implement the modified Bharat Net project statewide.
    *   The project, originally launched as the National Optical Fiber Mission in 2011, was renamed Bharat Net in 2015.
    *   Its primary objective is to provide high-speed internet connectivity.
    *   The modified project aims to enable surveillance of border areas from any part of the country.
    *   Optical fiber technology operates on the principle of Total Internal Reflection (TIR).

*   **India's First Megawatt-Hour Scale Vanadium Flow Battery**
    *   India's first megawatt-hour scale Vanadium Flow Battery was inaugurated at NTPC Netra (NTPC's Research and Development center) in Greater Noida.
    *   The battery has a capacity of 3 megawatts.
    *   It is intended to replace traditional Lithium-ion batteries and boost renewable energy storage.
    *   The inauguration was performed by Union Minister Manohar Lal Khattar (Minister of Housing and Urban Affairs).

*   **Real Craft Portal**
    *   The "Real Craft Portal" was launched by the Ministry of Fisheries, Animal Husbandry, and Dairying.
    *   The portal facilitates online licensing and registration of fishing vessels.
    *   It aims to enhance transparency, reduce corruption, and improve efficiency in the process.
    *   Separately, Cyber Bharat Setu has been established in Tripura.

*   **National Karting Championship**
    *   Nine-year-old Arshi Gupta from Faridabad became India's first female racer to win the National Karting Championship.
