### Union Budget 2026-27: Overview and Historical Context

*   **Presentation:** The Union Budget 2026-27 was presented by Finance Minister Nirmala Sitharaman on February 1, 2026.
*   **Tradition:** Before presenting the budget, the Finance Minister meets the President of India and follows the tradition of eating curd before commencing the task.
    *   **First Budget:** James Wilson presented the first budget in India in 1860.
    *   **Post-Independence:** R.K. Shanmukham Chetty presented the first budget of independent India in 1947.
    *   **Republic of India:** John Matthai was the first Finance Minister to present the budget for the Republic of India in 1950.
    *   **Most Budgets:** Morarji Desai holds the record for presenting the budget 10 times (though not consecutively).
    *   She is the first full-time female Finance Minister of India.
    *   She holds the record for the longest budget speech (2020), which lasted 2 hours and 40 minutes.
    *   She is the Finance Minister to present the budget the highest number of times consecutively (starting from 2019).
    *   The 2026-27 budget is her ninth budget presentation.
    *   Indira Gandhi presented budgets as a Prime Minister holding the additional charge of Finance Minister, but she was not a full-time Finance Minister.

### Budget 2026-27: Core Objectives and Requirements

*   **Economic Growth:** India is currently growing at a 7% growth rate. Reasons cited include energy security, reduced import dependency, structural reforms, fiscal prudence, monetary stability, emphasis on public investment, and promotion of manufacturing.
*   **Three Duties:** The budget outlines three primary duties:
    1.  Rapid economic development.
    2.  Fulfilling the aspirations of the people.
    3.  Inclusive development (Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas).
*   **Three Requirements:** To fulfill these duties, the government identified three requirements:
    1.  Improvement in infrastructure.
    2.  Strengthening the financial sector and banking.
    3.  Integration of Artificial Intelligence and technology.

### Reform Express

*   The government has initiated a "Reform Express" to boost employment generation, productivity, and economic growth.
*   Approximately 350 reforms have been implemented, including GST reforms and the implementation of labor codes.

### Six Pillars of Economic Development

To achieve the first duty of rapid economic development, the budget focuses on six pillars:
1.  Manufacturing.
2.  Heritage (related to MSMEs).
3.  Champion MSMEs (Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises).
4.  Infrastructure.
5.  Energy Security.
6.  Development of urban economic areas.
