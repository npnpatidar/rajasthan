### First Five-Year Plan (1951–1956)

*   **Duration:** Started on April 1, 1951, and continued until 1956.
*   **Model:** Based on the Harrod-Domar model.
*   **Drafting:** The draft was prepared by K.N. Raj.
*   **Focus:** Agriculture.
*   **Performance:** The target growth rate was 2.1%, while the achieved growth rate was 3.6%.

### Second Five-Year Plan (1956–1961)

*   **Duration:** 1956–1961.
*   **Model:** Based on the Mahalanobis model.
*   **Focus:** Heavy industries.
*   **Industrial Development:** Established iron and steel plants, including:
    *   Bhilai (Chhattisgarh)
    *   Rourkela (Odisha)
    *   Durgapur (West Bengal)
*   **Performance:** The target growth rate was 4.5%, while the achieved growth rate was 4.27%.

### Third Five-Year Plan (1961–1966)

*   **Duration:** 1961–1966.
*   **Model:** Based on the Sukhamoy Chakravarty model.
*   **Objective:** Focused on both agriculture and industry.
*   **Challenges:** The plan failed to meet its targets due to the 1962 war with China and the war with Pakistan.
    *   Death of Lal Bahadur Shastri.
    *   The slogan "Jai Jawan, Jai Kisan" was given.
    *   Establishment of the Food Corporation of India in 1965.
