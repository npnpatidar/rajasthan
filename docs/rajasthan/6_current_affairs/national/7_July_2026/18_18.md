# Transcript Summaries (170 chunks)

### Union and its Territory

* **Article 1:** Defines India as having 28 states and 8 Union Territories.
* **Article 2:** Deals with the admission or establishment of new states. Sikkim was added via the 36th Constitutional Amendment.
* **Article 3:** Empowers Parliament to change the name or boundaries of states, merge two states into one, or create a new state from an existing one. Examples include the renaming of Kerala to 'Keralam' and the formation of Telangana.

### Citizenship

* **Article 9:** If a person acquires the citizenship of another country, their Indian citizenship is automatically terminated.
* **Article 11:** Grants Parliament the power to legislate on matters related to citizenship.

### Judicial Review

* **Article 13:** Empowers the Supreme Court to strike down any law made by Parliament if it is inconsistent with fundamental rights. This is referred to as the power of judicial review.

### Right to Equality

* **Article 14:** Guarantees equality before the law and equal protection of laws.
* **Article 15(1):** Prohibits the state from discriminating against any citizen on the grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
* **Article 15(6):** Provides 10% reservation for EWS (Economically Weaker Sections), introduced via the 103rd Constitutional Amendment.
* **Article 16:** Deals with equality of opportunity in public employment and includes the 10% EWS reservation.
* **Article 17:** Abolishes untouchability.
* **Article 18:** Abolishes titles, with exceptions for military and academic distinctions.

### Right to Freedom

* **Article 19:** Guarantees freedom of speech and expression.
    * **19(1)(a):** Freedom of speech and expression, which includes the right to speak and the right to remain silent.
    * **19(1)(b):** Right to assemble peacefully and without arms.
    * **19(1)(c):** Right to form associations/unions.
    * **19(1)(d):** Right to move freely throughout India.
    * **19(1)(e):** Right to reside in any part of India.
    * **19(1)(f):** Right to property (repealed by the 44th Constitutional Amendment, 1978).
    * **19(1)(g):** Right to practice any profession or occupation.
* **Article 20:** Provides protection in respect of conviction for offenses. No person shall be punished twice for the same offense, and no person shall be compelled to be a witness against themselves.
* **Article 21:** Guarantees the right to life and personal liberty. Notable cases include:
    * **Maneka Gandhi case:** Right to travel abroad.
    * **Puttaswamy case:** Right to privacy.
    * **Navtej Kaur case:** Decriminalization of consensual same-sex relations.
* **Article 21A:** Right to education, added by the 86th Constitutional Amendment (2002).
* **Article 22:** Provides protection against arrest and detention.
* **Article 25:** Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion. Conversion is not an offense, but forced conversion is.
* **Article 26:** Freedom to manage religious affairs.
* **Article 27:** No taxes shall be levied for the promotion of any particular religion.
* **Article 28:** Regulates attendance at religious instruction or worship in certain educational institutions.

### Right against Exploitation

* **Article 23:** Prohibits human trafficking and forced labor (begar).
* **Article 24:** Prohibits the employment of children in factories.

### Cultural and Educational Rights

* **Article 29:** Protection of interests of minorities, including their language, script, and culture.
* **Article 30:** Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions.

### Constitutional Remedies

* **Article 32:** Right to constitutional remedies. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar called this the "soul of the Constitution." The Supreme Court can issue five types of writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Quo Warranto, Certiorari, and Prohibition.

### Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP)

* **Article 37:** States that if DPSP is violated, one cannot go to court.
* **Article 38:** Promotes the welfare of the people by securing a social order.
* **Article 39(a):** Ensures that men and women have the right to an adequate means of livelihood.
* **Article 39(d):** Equal pay for equal work for both men and women.
* **Article 39A:** Free legal aid (added by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment). This led to the establishment of NALSA (National Legal Services Authority) in 1995.
* **Article 40:** Organization of village panchayats.
* **Article 41:** Right to work, education, and public assistance.
* **Article 42:** Provision for maternity relief.
* **Article 43:** Management of industries.
* **Article 43B:** Promotion of cooperative societies (added by the 97th Constitutional Amendment).
* **Article 44:** Uniform Civil Code. States that have passed/brought bills include Uttarakhand, Gujarat, and Assam.
* **Article 48:** Organization of agriculture and animal husbandry.

### Fundamental Duties

* **Article 51A:** Fundamental duties, added by the 42nd Constitutional Amendment on the recommendation of the Sardar Swaran Singh Committee. Originally 10 duties; the 11th duty (education for children aged 6-14) was added by the 86th Constitutional Amendment (2002).

### Hydrogen for Heritage Project

### New Education Policy (NEP) 2020

* **Implementation Date:** The policy was implemented on July 29, 2020.
* **Language Policy Recommendation:** The Supreme Court has advised that the third language (foreign language or English) should be introduced from the 5th or 6th grade rather than the 9th grade.
* **Rationale:** Introducing a third language in the 9th or 10th grade increases mental stress on students and is considered a mere formality, as students focus primarily on achieving high percentages in exams rather than learning the language.
* **Historical Context:** The first education policy in India was introduced in 1968, followed by another in 1986.

### Education: Constitutional Status

* **List Classification:** Education was originally a subject under the State List at the time the Constitution was drafted.
* **Amendment:** It was transferred to the Concurrent List via the 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act of 1976.
* **Transferred Subjects:** The 42nd Amendment moved five subjects from the State List to the Concurrent List:
    * Education
    * Forests
    * Wildlife
    * Weights and measures
    * Administration of justice
