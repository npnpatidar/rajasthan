### Constitutional Provisions: Articles 1–3

*   **Article 1:** Defines India as having 28 states and 8 Union Territories.
*   **Article 2:** Pertains to the admission or establishment of new states. Sikkim was added via the 36th Constitutional Amendment.
*   **Article 3:** Empowers Parliament to change the name or boundaries of states, merge two states into one, or create a new state from an existing one. Examples include the renaming of Kerala to "Keralam" and the formation of Telangana.

### Citizenship (Articles 9 and 11)

*   **Article 9:** If a person acquires citizenship of another country, their Indian citizenship is automatically terminated.
*   **Article 11:** Grants Parliament the power to legislate on all matters related to citizenship.

### Judicial Review (Article 13)

*   **Article 13:** Empowers the Supreme Court to strike down any law made by Parliament if it is inconsistent with fundamental rights. This is referred to as the power of judicial review.

### Right to Equality (Articles 14–18)

*   **Article 14:** Covers equality before the law and equal protection of laws.
*   **Article 15(1):** Prohibits the state from discriminating against any citizen on grounds of religion, race, caste, sex, or place of birth.
*   **Article 15(6):** Provides 10% reservation for EWS (Economically Weaker Sections), introduced via the 103rd Constitutional Amendment.
*   **Article 16:** Deals with equality of opportunity in public employment; it also includes the 10% EWS reservation.
*   **Article 17:** Abolishes untouchability.
*   **Article 18:** Abolishes titles, with exceptions for military and academic distinctions.

### Right to Freedom (Articles 19–22)

*   **Article 19:** Guarantees freedom of speech and expression.
    *   **19(1)(a):** Freedom of speech and expression, which includes the right to speak and the right to remain silent.
    *   **19(1)(b):** Right to assemble peacefully and without arms.
    *   **19(1)(c):** Right to form associations/unions.
    *   **19(1)(d):** Right to move freely throughout India.
    *   **19(1)(e):** Right to reside in any part of India.
    *   **19(1)(f):** Right to property (repealed by the 44th Constitutional Amendment, 1978).
    *   **19(1)(g):** Right to practice any profession or occupation.
*   **Article 20:** Provides protection in respect of conviction for offenses. No person shall be punished for the same offense more than once, and no person shall be compelled to be a witness against themselves.
*   **Article 21:** Guarantees the right to life and personal liberty. Judicial interpretations include the right to travel abroad (Maneka Gandhi case), the right to privacy (Puttaswamy case), and the decriminalization of consensual same-sex relations (Navtej Singh Johar case).
*   **Article 21A:** Right to education, added by the 86th Constitutional Amendment, 2002.
*   **Article 22:** Provides protection against arrest and detention.

### Right Against Exploitation (Articles 23–24)

*   **Article 23:** Prohibits human trafficking and forced labor (begar).
*   **Article 24:** Prohibits the employment of children in factories.

### Right to Freedom of Religion (Articles 25–28)

*   **Article 25:** Freedom of conscience and free profession, practice, and propagation of religion. Conversion is not an offense, but forced conversion is.
*   **Article 26:** Freedom to manage religious affairs.
*   **Article 27:** No taxes shall be levied for the promotion of any particular religion.
*   **Article 28:** Regulates attendance at religious instruction or worship in certain educational institutions.

### Cultural and Educational Rights (Articles 29–30)

*   **Article 29:** Protection of interests of minorities, including their language, script, and culture.
*   **Article 30:** Right of minorities to establish and administer educational institutions.

### Constitutional Remedies (Article 32)

*   **Article 32:** Right to constitutional remedies. Dr. B.R. Ambedkar described this as the "heart and soul" of the Constitution. The Supreme Court can issue five types of writs: Habeas Corpus, Mandamus, Quo-Warranto, Certiorari, and Prohibition.

### Dengue Virus Transmission

*   **Pathogen:** The disease is caused by a virus.
*   **Vector:** The female Aedes aegypti (mada Aedes aegypti) mosquito acts as the carrier/vector for the virus.

### Dengue Virus Control Measures

*   **Vector:** The female *Aedes aegypti* mosquito acts as the carrier (vector) for the dengue virus.
*   **Breeding Habitat:** The mosquito lays larvae in clean and stagnant water.
    *   **Biological Control:** Using *Gambusia* fish, which consume mosquito larvae.
    *   **Chemical Control:** Pouring kerosene (kerosene oil) into the water. This reduces the surface tension of the water, causing the larvae to drown and die.
