### Constitutional Amendment Process

*   **Constitutional Provision:** The procedure for constitutional amendment is mentioned in Part 20 of the Constitution.
*   **Source:** The provision for amendment was adopted from the South African Constitution.
*   **Article:** Article 368 deals with the amendment of the Constitution.
*   **Legislative Procedure:** An amendment bill is introduced in Parliament. Once passed by Parliament and signed by the President, the bill becomes an Act.
*   **Current Status:** As of the lecture, 106 constitutional amendment acts have been passed.
*   **Recent Bill:** The 129th Constitutional Amendment Bill was introduced, which pertains to "One Nation, One Election."

### 1st Constitutional Amendment Act (1951)

*   **Key Provision:** Added the 9th Schedule to the Constitution.
*   **Subject:** Related to land reforms.
*   **Prime Minister:** Jawaharlal Nehru was the Prime Minister at the time.

### 7th Constitutional Amendment Act (1956)

*   **Subject:** Related to the State Reorganization Commission.

### 21st Constitutional Amendment Act (1967)

*   **Key Provision:** Added the 15th language to the 8th Schedule of the Constitution.
*   **Language Added:** Sindhi.

### 26th Constitutional Amendment Act (1971)

*   **Key Provision:** Abolished the "Privy Purse," which was a pension-like amount given to former kings and maharajas.
*   **Prime Minister:** Indira Gandhi was the Prime Minister at the time.

### 36th Constitutional Amendment Act (1975)

*   **Key Provision:** Granted full statehood to Sikkim.

### 42nd Constitutional Amendment Act (1976)

*   **Alternative Name:** Known as the "Mini Constitution" or "Lagu Samvidhan."
*   **Preamble:** Amended for the first time to include three words: Socialist (Samajwad), Secular (Panth-nirpekshata), and Integrity (Akhandata).
*   **Fundamental Duties:** Added Part 4A and Article 51A, based on the recommendations of the Sardar Swaran Singh Committee.
    *   Article 39A: Added to provide free legal aid.
    *   Article 48A: Added to address environmental and forest protection.
*   **Tribunals:** Added Part 14A, which includes Articles 323A and 323B, related to tribunals (Nyayadhikaran).
*   **List Transfers:** Five subjects were moved from the State List to the Concurrent List: Education, Forests, Wildlife, Weights and Measures, and Administration of Justice.

### 44th Constitutional Amendment Act (1978)

*   **Key Provision:** Removed the "Right to Property" from the category of Fundamental Rights.
*   **Current Status:** It was made a legal/statutory right under Article 300A.

### 52nd Constitutional Amendment Act (1985)

*   **Key Provision:** Added the 10th Schedule to the Constitution.
*   **Subject:** Anti-defection law.
*   **Prime Minister:** Rajiv Gandhi was the Prime Minister at the time.

### 61st Constitutional Amendment Act (1989)

*   **Key Provision:** Reduced the voting age from 21 years to 18 years.
*   **Prime Minister:** Rajiv Gandhi was the Prime Minister at the time.

### 69th Constitutional Amendment Act (1991)

*   **Subject:** Related to Delhi.
*   **Key Provision:** Inserted Article 239AA.
*   **Ministerial Limit:** While the general constitutional limit for ministers is 15%, for Delhi, the limit is 10%.

### 70th Constitutional Amendment Act (1992)

*   **Key Provision:** Provided that elected MLAs from Delhi and Puducherry shall participate in the Presidential election.

### 71st Constitutional Amendment Act (1992)

*   **Key Provision:** Added three new languages to the 8th Schedule: Nepali, Manipuri, and Konkani.
*   **Total Languages:** With this addition, the total number of languages in the 8th Schedule reached 18.

### 73rd Constitutional Amendment Act (1992)

*   **Implementation Date:** Came into force on April 24, 1993.
    *   Added Part 9 to the Constitution.
    *   Added Articles 243 to 243O.
    *   Added the 11th Schedule.
*   **Subject Matter:** Relates to Panchayati Raj or rural self-governance/local government.
*   **Schedule 11:** Contains 29 subjects.

### 74th Constitutional Amendment Act

*   **Subject Matter:** Relates to urban bodies (Nagar Nikay).
    *   Added Part 9A.
    *   Added the 12th Schedule.

### 86th Constitutional Amendment Act (2002)

*   **Right to Education:** Added Article 21A, making education a Fundamental Right.
*   **Fundamental Duties:** Added the 11th fundamental duty under Article 51A.

### 89th Constitutional Amendment Act (2003)

*   **National Commission for Scheduled Tribes (NCST):** Established the commission and inserted Article 338A.

### 91st Constitutional Amendment Act (2003)

*   **Council of Ministers:** Fixed the size of the Council of Ministers at 15% of the total membership of the Lok Sabha (at the Centre) and the Legislative Assembly (in the States).

### 92nd Constitutional Amendment Act (2003)

*   **8th Schedule:** Added four new languages: Bodo, Dogri, Maithili, and Santhali.
*   **Total Languages:** The total number of languages in the 8th Schedule reached 22.

### 97th Constitutional Amendment Act (2011)

*   **Cooperative Societies:** Added Part 9B related to cooperative societies.
*   **Article 43B:** Inserted into the Constitution.

### 99th Constitutional Amendment Act (2014)

*   **National Judicial Appointments Commission (NJAC):** Created the commission for judicial appointments.
*   **Judicial Review:** The Supreme Court declared this amendment unconstitutional and rejected it, making it the only constitutional amendment to be struck down by the Supreme Court.

### 100th Constitutional Amendment Act (2015)

*   **Three Bigha Corridor:** Related to the agreement between India and Bangladesh.

### 101st Constitutional Amendment Act

*   **GST:** Relates to Goods and Services Tax.

### 103rd Constitutional Amendment Act (2019)

*   **EWS Reservation:** Provided 10% reservation for the Economically Weaker Section (EWS).
*   **Constitutional Additions:** Inserted Articles 15(6) and 16(6).

### 104th Constitutional Amendment Act

*   **Reservation Extension:** Extended the reservation for SC and ST seats in the Lok Sabha and Legislative Assemblies for another 10 years.
*   **Anglo-Indian Reservation:** Abolished the reservation for Anglo-Indians (which previously consisted of two seats in the Lok Sabha and one seat in the Legislative Assemblies).

### 105th Constitutional Amendment Act

*   **OBC Identification:** Clarified that the responsibility to determine which castes are included in the OBC category lies with the State.
*   **Context:** This amendment relates to the 102nd Constitutional Amendment, which had established the National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC) and inserted Article 338B.

### 106th Constitutional Amendment Act

*   **Nari Shakti Vandan Adhiniyam:** Relates to the reservation of seats for women.
*   **Implementation:** The reservation will be implemented after the delimitation process following a census.

### 129th Constitutional Amendment Bill

*   Introduced in Parliament, this bill relates to "One Nation, One Election" and is associated with the Ramnath Kovind Committee.
*   **Status:** The bill has been introduced in Parliament; the Ramnath Kovind Committee was associated with this.
