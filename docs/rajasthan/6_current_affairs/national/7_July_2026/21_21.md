### First Five-Year Plan (1951–1956)

*   **Duration:** Started on April 1, 1951, and continued until 1956.
*   **Model:** Based on the Harad-Domar model.
*   **Drafting:** The draft was prepared by K.N. Raj.
*   **Focus:** Agriculture.
*   **Performance:** The target growth rate was 2.1%, and the achieved growth rate was 3.6%.

### Second Five-Year Plan (1956–1961)

*   **Duration:** 1956–1961.
*   **Model:** Based on the Mahalanobis model.
*   **Focus:** Industries, specifically heavy industries.
*   **Industrial Development:** Establishment of iron and steel plants:
    *   **Bhilai:** Located in Chhattisgarh.
    *   **Rourkela:** Located in Odisha.
    *   **Durgapur:** Located in West Bengal.
*   **Performance:** The target growth rate was 4.5%, and the achieved growth rate was 4.27%.

### Third Five-Year Plan (1961–1966)

*   **Duration:** 1961–1966.
*   **Model:** Based on the Sukhamoy Chakravarty model.
*   **Focus:** Both agriculture and industry.
*   **Challenges:** The plan failed to meet its targets due to the 1962 China war and the 1965 India-Pakistan war.
    *   Death of Lal Bahadur Shastri.
    *   The slogan "Jai Jawan, Jai Kisan" was given.
    *   Establishment of the Food Corporation of India in 1965.

### Plan Holiday (1966–1969)

*   **Duration:** Three years (1966–67, 1967–68, 1968–69).
*   **Nature:** One-year plans were implemented during this period.
*   **Green Revolution:** Initiated during this time to achieve self-reliance in food grains.
*   **Duration:** 1990–1992.
*   **Economic Context:** During this period, India faced a foreign exchange reserve crisis, resulting in a lack of foreign currency to purchase goods.
*   **New Economic Policy (1991):** Under Prime Minister P.V. Narasimha Rao and Finance Minister Dr. Manmohan Singh, the LPG (Liberalization, Privatization, and Globalization) model was adopted.

*   **Duration:** 1969–1974.
*   **Model:** Based on the Ashok Rudra model; the draft was prepared by D.R. Gadgil.
