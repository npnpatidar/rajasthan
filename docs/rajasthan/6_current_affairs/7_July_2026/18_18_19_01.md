# India Semiconductor Mission (ISM)

### Overview
* **Launch:** Launched in 2021 under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY).
* **Objective:** To build a vibrant semiconductor and display ecosystem, positioning India as a global hub for electronics manufacturing and design.
* **Nodal Agency:** India Semiconductor Mission (ISM), a specialized and independent business division within the Digital India Corporation.

### Key Schemes under ISM
* **Scheme for setting up of Semiconductor Fabs:** Financial support to eligible applicants for setting up Silicon semiconductor fabrication plants.
* **Scheme for setting up of Display Fabs:** Fiscal support for setting up TFT LCD / AMOLED-based display fabrication units.
* **Scheme for Compound Semiconductors / Silicon Photonics / Sensors Fab and Semiconductor Assembly, Testing, Marking and Packaging (ATMP) / OSAT facilities:** Offers financial support of 50% of capital expenditure to approved applicants.
* **Design Linked Incentive (DLI) Scheme:** Offers financial incentives, design infrastructure support, and deployment linkages across various stages of semiconductor design development.

### Financial Incentives & Funding Structure
* **Total Outlay:** ₹76,000 crore (~USD 10 billion).
* **Uniform Fiscal Support:** The government provides a uniform **50% fiscal support** of the project cost for semiconductor fabs, display fabs, and packaging units (ATMP/OSAT) across all technology nodes.

### Strategic Significance
* **Reducing Import Dependency:** Reduces reliance on imports from dominant markets like Taiwan, China, and South Korea.
* **Supply Chain Resilience:** Safeguards key domestic industries (automotive, telecom, medical devices) against global chip shortage disruptions.
* **Geopolitical Alignment:** Complements bilateral pacts such as the US-India Initiative on Critical and Emerging Technology (iCET) and the India-EU Trade and Technology Council (TTC).
* **Economic Multiplier:** Boosts domestic electronics manufacturing, creates high-skilled jobs, and encourages domestic intellectual property (IP) generation.

### Key Implementation Challenges
* **High Capital Intensity:** Continuous capital injection is required to keep pace with rapid technology obsolescence.
* **Infrastructure Bottlenecks:** Fabs require millions of gallons of ultra-pure water daily and uninterrupted, fluctuation-free power supply.
* **Specialized Talent Gap:** High demand for highly specialized workforce in microelectronics, chemical engineering, and VLSI (Very Large Scale Integration) design.
* **Ecosystem Absence:** Lack of a local supply chain for raw materials such as high-purity specialty gases, chemicals, and silicon wafers.
