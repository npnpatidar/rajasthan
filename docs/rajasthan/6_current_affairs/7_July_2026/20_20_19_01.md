# India's Semiconductor Mission (ISM)

### Overview
* **Nodal Ministry:** Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY).
* **Total Outlay:** ₹76,000 crore (~$10 billion).
* **Objective:** To build a vibrant semiconductor and display design ecosystem, positioning India as a global hub for electronics manufacturing and design.

### Key Schemes under ISM

| Scheme Component | Target / Eligibility | Fiscal Support |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Semiconductor Fabs** | Silicon-based semiconductor fabrication plants | **50% of Project Cost** on equal pari-passu basis |
| **Display Fabs** | TFT LCD / AMOLED based display panels | **50% of Project Cost** on equal pari-passu basis |
| **Compound Semiconductors / OSAT** | Compound Semiconductors, Silicon Photonics, Sensors, ATMP/OSAT facilities | **50% of Capital Expenditure** on pari-passu basis |
| **Design Linked Incentive (DLI)** | Domestic semiconductor design companies | Up to **50% of eligible expenditure** (subject to a ceiling of ₹15 Crore per application) |

### Strategic Significance
* **Supply Chain Resilience:** Minimizes vulnerability to global supply chain disruptions (e.g., shortages experienced during COVID-19).
* **National Security:** Secures critical infrastructure, defense systems, and telecommunications from hardware-level vulnerabilities and cyber threats.
* **Import Substitution:** Reduces heavy reliance on semiconductor imports, primarily from Taiwan, China, and South Korea.

# COP28 UAE Consensus (Global Climate Action)

### Key Decisions and Agreements

* **Transitioning Away from Fossil Fuels:** 
  * Landmark agreement to transition away from fossil fuels in energy systems in a "just, orderly, and equitable manner."
  * Accelerating action in this critical decade to achieve net-zero emissions by 2050.
* **Loss and Damage Fund (LDF):**
  * Officially operationalized on Day 1 of the summit to assist vulnerable developing nations facing severe impacts of climate change.
  * **Initial Pledges:** Over $700 million committed by various nations (including UAE, Germany, Italy, and France).
  * **Host:** Managed temporarily by the World Bank for an interim period of 4 years.
* **Global Targets for 2030:**
  * **Renewable Energy:** Tripling global renewable energy capacity to at least 11,000 GW.
  * **Energy Efficiency:** Doubling the global average annual rate of energy efficiency improvements from ~2% to over 4%.
* **Declaration on Climate and Health:** 
  * Signed by over 120 countries to address the growing health impacts of climate change (e.g., extreme heat, infectious diseases).

### Key Challenges & Concerns
* **Financial Gaps:** Lack of legally binding commitments or clear pathways for mobilising the trillions of dollars needed for developing nations to transition.
* **Abatement Technologies Clause:** Criticism over loopholes regarding "abatement and removal technologies" (like Carbon Capture and Storage), which critics argue may prolong the use of fossil fuels.
